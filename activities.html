AI and Networking Lab (DAPHNE)




Biography

Dr. Kiran is a Computer Scientist in the Scientific Networking Division, housed in the Prototypes and Testbed group at ESnet, Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory. She currently leads the AI for networking lab building solutions for operational network research and engineering problems. She received her Ph.D. and MSc (Eng) in Computer Science from University of Sheffield in 2011, 2009, respectively. Before joining the lab in 2016, Kiran worked at various Universities of Sheffield, Leeds, and University College London, with collaborations with European industries such as SAP, ATOS, and BT. Her research explores deployable machine learning and decentralized optimization for wide area networks, wireless, and Cloud infrastructures, such as building self-driving networks. She also helped develop FLAME, an open-source agent-based solution used world-wide for complexity research.

Her awards include the Royal Society Scientist in Westminster in 2015, 2017 U.S. DOE Early Career Award, and N2Women Rising Stars Award in 2021. She is an active member of ACM and a Senior IEEE communities.
