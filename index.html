Mariam Kiran

Computational Research Scientist

Lawrence Berkeley National Lab

I am part of two research groups: Energy Science Network (ESnet) and Scientific Data Management Research (CRD).

My research involves working with high performance computing and networking issues. Currently, Im developing decentralized learning techniques (e.g. using deep learning algorithms) to build agents that can improve operational performances of distributed computing and networks. Mainly concentrated on networks, I have been working with grids, GPUs and Cloud computing for improving performance of complex data-intensive science applications.

In the past I worked on cloud and grid computing, applying multi-agent and complex systems with evolutionary learning algorithms.

Before moving to USA, I was an Asst. Prof. in Software Engineering at University of Bradford, UK. Before that I worked as a PostDoc at Universities of Leeds and Sheffield in the UK.

I received my bachelors, MSc(Eng) and PhD from the University of Sheffield, UK, working with Professor Mike Holcombe as my thesis advisor. Old pages at Sheffield
