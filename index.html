Mariam Kiran

Computational Research Scientist

Lawrence Berkeley National Lab

I am part of two research groups: Energy Science Network (ESnet) and Scientific Data Management Research (CRD).

My research focuses on improving performance of distributed networks and computing facilities for high-performance applications. Currently, Im developing decentralized learning techniques (e.g. such as using deep learning algorithms) to build agents to improve operational performances of distributed networks. I am exploring scaling techniques on grids, GPUs and Cloud computing for improving performance of complex data-intensive science applications.

In the past, I used evolutionary learning in multi-agent for improving cloud and grid computing.

Before moving to US, I was an Asst. Prof. in Software Engineering at University of Bradford, UK. Prior, I was a PostDoc at Universities of Leeds and Sheffield (UK).

I received my MSc(Eng) and PhD from University of Sheffield, UK, working with Professor Mike Holcombe as my thesis advisor. Old pages at Sheffield
