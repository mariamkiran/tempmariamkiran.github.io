As a staff scientist, my research sits at the intersection of using data to improve performance of distributed networks and computing facilities, to enable high-performance science applications in astrophysics and bioinformatics. I belong to two research groups:

  • Scientific Data Management Research (@ CRD)
  • Advanced Network Technologies (@ Energy Science Network (ESnet))

My research interests include decentralized machine learning techniques for reinforcement and near real-time agent reaction, parallel computing, workflow improvements and high performance networks. Previously, I have investigated scaling techniques on grids, GPUs and Cloud computing for data-intensive simulations and science applications. My work has been involved with testing and developing platforms with e-science Grids at Rutherford Appleton Labs (Oxford), Amazon AWS and OpenStack. Before moving to the U.S., I was an Asst. Prof. at the University of Bradford (in U.K.). I received my PhD from the University of Sheffield, U.K., working with Professor Mike Holcombe as my thesis advisor.

I am the recipient of the DOE Early Career Award in 2017 and have previously held fellowships such as a Royal Society 'Scientists in Westminister' and EPSRC promising early career scientists. I also serve as an EU reviewer for Marie Sklodowska-Curie fellowships and Horizon2020 ICT calls. I am also an elected board member with the CoMSES Network for OpenABM models serving for developing open agent-based models and frameworks. I was one of the developers of the FLAME framework which automatically parallelizes complex simulation models on Grids. I am an ACM member.
