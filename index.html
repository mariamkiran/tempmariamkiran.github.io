I am currently developing Artificial Intelligence and Control algorithms to advance distributed computing and high-performance networks.
I am a Research Scientist at Energy Science Network (ESnet) at LBNL, Berkeley.
Before LBNL in 2016, I held academic and postdoctoral positions at Universities Sheffield, Leeds and Bradford. My research concentrates on working with large distributed computing challenges such as high-performance networks, computing and Clouds. I have contributed to a number of notable projects, such as developing FLAME (open-source software for Agent based modelling on Grid and GPU). Such as simulating economic market behaviour.

I have developed solutions for OpenStack Cloud architectures, Intent-based networking and adding AI to SDN controllers. I earned my undergraduate and postgraduate degrees from University of Sheffield, UK, and PhD in Economic and Evolutionary learning for Agents on High-performance computing in 2011.

I have won a number of awards such as Scientist for Westminster (UK parliament), best engineering dissertation award, EPSRC international award and recently the ASCR DOE Early career award in 2017 for “Intelligent Large-scale Deep Learning for High-Performance Networks”. I have a number of research grants in Control and AI in distributed computing, such as:

  • Real-time and streaming machine learning algorithms
  • Decentralized Agent Computing for distributed AI for edge devices (network, IoT)
  • Deep Reinforcement learning for real-world applications
  • Time series and prediction algorithms
