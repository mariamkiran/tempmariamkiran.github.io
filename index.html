I am a Research Scientist at Energy Science Network (ESnet) at LBNL based in Berkeley, California. My research interests are working on Artificial Intelligence and Control algorithms to advance Distributed and High-Performance Networks, working on traffic classification and engineering. My key interests are distributed science workflows, network measurements, wide area networks, and recently sensor networks. My research background stems from high-performance computing, cloud computing and machine learning. I have developed solutions for OpenStack Cloud architectures, Intent-based networking and adding AI to SDN controllers. My motivation is to bring machine learning to network research challenges. I was awarded the ASCR DOE Early Career Award in 2017 to do this work DAPHNE Project “Intelligent Large-scale Deep Learning for High-Performance Networks”. I also have held awards for Royal Society's Scientist for Westminster (UK parliament), Fretwell-Downing Prize at Sheffield and EPSRC early career award.

I was the co-developer for notable projects FLAME (scalable open-source software for Agent based modeling on Grid and GPU) and EURACE, modelling the complete European Economy markets, known as an agent-based modeling expert in UK, European and USA research circles in complexity sciences. I have worked on distributed cloud computing as postdoc at University of Leeds, Sheffield and Bradford, on projects such as EU EURACE, EU OPTIMIS, EU Broker@Cloud. I received my PhD from University of Sheffield, UK, in Evolutionary learning for Intelligent Agents on High-performance computing in 2011 under the supervision of Emeritus Professor Mike Holcombe.

I am focused on developing a research group for deep learning for infrastructure research area at Lawrence Berkeley National Lab. If you are interested, please feel free to contact me.
